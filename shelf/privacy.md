# Privacy Policy — Shelf

**Effective:** May 4, 2026

## What we collect

- The books you add to your shelves (title, author, ISBN, cover URL, your status, your notes).
- Reading sessions you log (date only).
- Your subscription status, managed by Apple.

We do not collect names, email addresses, contact lists, location, or device identifiers.

## How we use it

- Your shelf data lives in a local SwiftData store on your device.
- When you search by title or scan a barcode, we query **Open Library** (run by the non-profit Internet Archive) to fetch book metadata and cover images. Open Library does not require an API key and does not track users.

## Third-party services

- **Open Library / Internet Archive** — the public API we query for book metadata. No tracking. https://openlibrary.org
- **Apple StoreKit** — subscription status.

If you tap a "Buy" button, we open a Bookshop.org URL in your browser. Bookshop.org's privacy policy applies once you leave Shelf. See https://bookshop.org/info/privacy.

No analytics, ads, or third-party trackers in Shelf itself.

## Camera permission

The barcode scanner uses your camera locally. We don't transmit camera frames anywhere — only the decoded ISBN string is sent to Open Library.

## Storage

All shelf data lives on your device. Nothing is uploaded to us (we do not run servers).

## Children

Shelf is not directed at children under 13.

## Changes

If our practices change, we will update this page.
